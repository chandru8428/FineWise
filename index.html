💳 Credit Cards

Everything you need to know to use credit cards smartly, build your CIBIL score, and avoid common traps.

Beginner Friendly

What is a Credit Card?

A credit card is a payment instrument issued by a bank or NBFC that allows you to borrow up to a sanctioned limit to make purchases online and offline. It lets you spend using the issuer’s credit and repay later within the billing cycle.

RBI Guidelines: You get a free credit period, but interest applies if the unpaid balance remains after the due date. Paying only the minimum amount due can stretch repayment over years and cause heavy interest costs.

✅ Do

  • Pay full bill on time
  • Use for planned purchases
  • Earn rewards on spending
  • Keep utilization below 30%

    • ❌ Don't

  • Withdraw cash from CC
  • Pay only minimum due
  • Overspend beyond budget
  • Apply for many cards at once

    How to Select a Credit Card

    Evaluate based on your monthly spending habits and eligibility criteria. Check fees, charges, and the interest rate before applying.

    1. Cashback vs Rewards

    Cashback cards are direct and simple. Rewards cards give points for lifestyle spending (dining, online shopping, groceries).

    2. Travel Benefits

    Look for air miles, zero forex markup, and complimentary airport lounge access if you travel frequently.

    3. Fuel Expenses

    If you commute daily, a co-branded fuel card helps save on fuel surcharges and gives reward points at petrol pumps.

    Checklist: Review joining fees, annual charges (and waiver conditions), interest rates, grace periods, and late payment charges before applying. Make sure it matches your primary use case!

    📈 Mutual Funds & SIP

    Learn how to invest smartly, understand different fund types, and use SIP to build long-term wealth.

    Wealth Building

    Understanding Investments

    1. What is a Mutual Fund?

    A mutual fund pools money from many investors and invests it in equities, bonds, or money market instruments, professionally managed by a fund manager.

    2. What is an ETF?

    Exchange-Traded Funds track indexes (like Nifty 50) and trade on exchanges just like stocks. They have real-time pricing and generally lower expense ratios than active mutual funds.

    3. What is a Debt Fund?

    Debt funds invest in fixed-income instruments like corporate bonds, government securities, and treasury bills. They are less volatile than equity but carry credit and interest rate risks.

    4. What is Digital Gold?

    Digital gold lets you buy small fractions of physical gold online, stored securely in vaults. Caution: SEBI does not currently regulate digital gold, and you usually pay a ~3% GST markup on purchases.

    Fund Terminology

    NAV (Net Asset Value)
    The price of one unit of a mutual fund. NAV = (Total fund assets - liabilities) ÷ Number of units. Changes daily based on market performance.
    AUM (Assets Under Management)
    Total value of money the fund manages. Larger AUM generally = more established fund. But too large can limit mid/small cap funds.

    What is SIP?

    SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) means automatically investing a fixed amount in a mutual fund every month — regardless of market conditions.

    Rupee Cost Averaging: When markets fall, your SIP buys more units at lower prices. When markets rise, fewer units at higher prices. This averages your cost over time.

    How to Select a Mutual Fund

    A mutual fund should be chosen based on your goal, time horizon, and risk appetite, not just by looking at past returns.

    Step 1

    Start with your Goal

    Equity funds for long-term growth, debt funds for stability, hybrid funds for a mix, and passive index funds for low-cost investing.

    Step 2

    Choose Direct Growth

    Always pick "Direct" plans over "Regular" plans to avoid distribution commissions. Pick "Growth" instead of "IDCW/Dividend" to benefit from compounding.

    Step 3

    Check Consistency

    Use rolling returns instead of point-to-point returns to see how the fund performs consistently across different market cycles.

    Key Metrics for Selecting Funds

    Expense Ratio
    Annual fee charged by fund (%). Lower is better. Index funds: 0.1-0.3%. Active funds: up to 2%.
    CAGR (Returns)
    Compounded Annual Growth Rate. Compare 3-year and 5-year CAGR against benchmark index.
    Rolling Returns
    Consistent returns over different time periods. More reliable than point-to-point returns.
    Alpha
    How much the fund outperforms its benchmark. Positive alpha = fund manager adding value.
    Beta
    Market sensitivity. Beta of 1 = moves same as market. Less than 1 = less volatile than market.
    Sharpe Ratio
    Risk-adjusted return. Higher is better. Shows how much return you get per unit of risk taken.
    Beginner Tip: Don't overanalyze. Start with 1-2 index funds (Nifty 50 + Nifty Next 50). Keep it simple, stay consistent, and invest for the long term.

    💸 Debt Payoff Planner

    Learn the smartest strategies to become debt-free and track your repayment progress.

    Debt Freedom
    ❄️

    Debt Snowball

    Pay smallest debt first. Get quick wins to stay motivated.

    Psychologically Motivated
    1. List debts smallest to largest balance
    2. Pay minimum on all except smallest
    3. Throw extra money at smallest debt
    4. Roll payment to next when paid off
    Best for: People who need motivation & visible progress
    🌊

    Debt Avalanche

    Pay highest interest debt first. Saves the most money.

    Mathematically Optimal
    1. List debts highest to lowest interest rate
    2. Pay minimum on all debts
    3. Put ALL extra money at highest-rate debt
    4. Roll payment to next when paid off
    Best for: Disciplined people focused on saving interest
